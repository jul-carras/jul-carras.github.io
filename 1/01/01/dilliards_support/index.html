Mapping Store Locations With ggmap

Published Jan 1, 0001 by Julian Carrasquillo

Mapping is a great way to get a sense of total market penetration or, like this example, for exploring potential opportunities. For a simple example, I’m going to focus on mapping Dilliard’s department stores - partly because it has plenty of stores for a nice data visualization, mostly because their store locator is SUPER web scraper friendly.

  • Dilliard’s locations were scraped from their Find a Store page on www.dilliards.com.
  • Nordstrom and Von Maur locations are those currently carrying Chaco product, per Sales Manager’s records.

  • Dilliard’s locations are concentrated in the south and southeast, with strong clusters in bigger cities outside of that region.
  • I think there is a good opportunity to pull for stores in the Southwest and Rockies region since the Dilliard’s consumer is different from the outdoor specialty crowd.
  • Florida and the Gulf Coast has strong representation, which can help us with Reach the Beach. OSM can get the smaller Surf accounts while High Plains gets the department store shopper.
